Average 1.5 to 2 Years of Learning

Beginning of year data.

PRIORITY:

Believing teaching is a profound act of leadership and that great teaching is necessary to ensure children realize their potential and have the full agency they need and deserve in life, we emphasize Learning to Teach (teacher leadership) and Learning to Lead (lifelong leadership for educational equity). We invest in supporting our leaders to build and bolster the necessary mindsets, knowledge, and skills to win with their kids in the classroom, and to understand, navigate, and ultimately dismantle the systemic barriers to educational excellence and equity.

LONG TERM OUTCOME:

We create proof points within our classrooms, and throughout our partnerships, that demonstrate the true capacity of our students in academics so that students in our states are college and career ready*. We generate exponential leadership in our network and states by positioning our students to explore and address the issues affecting their communities; ultimately, activating culturally-conscious, self-directed leaders-of-change engaged in thriving efforts to improve the conditions of our region and world.
*Students in our states see a statewide 95% graduation rate. Of that 95%, 19 of every 20 students achieve a 21 on ACT.

Annual Goal:

Students in our corps members’ classrooms get an average of 1.5 - 2 years of learning or better in one school year; every corps members’ classroom delivers a minimum of 1 year of learning.

The Greater Delta for a Greater Delta in 2021:

Leadership for Teaching and Leadership for Life

Vision

One day, all children of Mississippi and Arkansas will have access to an excellent education that illuminates their brilliance as leaders and learners, regardless of race, class, or any other identity-based factors.

Mission

We find, develop and support leaders to join the movement for educational equity and excellence within and beyond their classrooms in Mississippi and Arkansas. These leaders support and inspire each other to join Mississippians and Arkansans – in particular our students – in the ongoing movement to end educational inequity. We lead the way for excellent and equitable rural and small urban education, and act as a catalyst for progress across the United States.

Priorities

We are a network of leaders, grounded in understanding of Teach For America’s Theory of Leadership Development who ensure that those most affected by education inequity are centered in our work. Both independently and collectively, we assume agency and accountability to work in concert with others to:

  • Win in the classroom – position our kids to shine as Leaders and learners
  • Build deep, diverse, enduring relationships within the network, staff, schools and community
  • Elevate and expand a narrative of belief and possibility
